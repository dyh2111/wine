# Zinfandel Tasting Notes

## 2022 Michael David Earthquake Zinfandel
*Nose
Opens with earthy mushroom, ripe red fruit, rubber, and a slight phenolic note. 

The palate is smoother than expected, with light pepper spice, red fruit, and an almond-liqueur character, but the flavor feels somewhat diluted rather than concentrated.
Tannins are low, the texture is very smooth, and the mouthfeel is surprisingly light for a wine called “Earthquake.” 

Overall, pleasant and easy to drink, but lacking the depth, density, and complexity I expected from a higher-end Lodi Zin.

* My score: 89
* Would I Buy It Again? I prefer the Earthquake Cab over this.
